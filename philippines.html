Featured Festivals

10 Celebrations
January

Sinulog Festival

📍 Cebu City, Visayas

One of the grandest festivals in the Philippines, Sinulog honors the Santo Niño with vibrant street dancing, elaborate costumes, and a massive parade drawing millions of devotees and tourists.

Cultural · Religious · Street Dance ↗
January

Ati-Atihan Festival

📍 Kalibo, Aklan, Visayas

Known as the "Mother of All Philippine Festivals," Ati-Atihan features revelers painted in black soot dancing to drums in the streets, celebrating the Sto. Niño with wild, jubilant energy.

Cultural · Tribal · Music ↗
October

MassKara Festival

📍 Bacolod City, Visayas

Born from tragedy, MassKara is Bacolod's answer to adversity — a festival of smiling masks and exuberant street dancing that earned Bacolod its title as the "City of Smiles."

Art · Dance · Resilience ↗
May

Pahiyas Festival

📍 Lucban, Quezon, Luzon

A dazzling harvest festival where houses in Lucban are decorated with colorful kiping rice wafers, fruits, and vegetables in thanksgiving to San Isidro Labrador, patron of farmers.

Harvest · Folk Art · Colors ↗
August

Kadayawan Festival

📍 Davao City, Mindanao

Davao's week-long celebration of life, nature's bounty, and the rich heritage of its 11 indigenous tribes, highlighted by floral parades, street dancing, and cultural showcases.

Indigenous · Flowers · Heritage ↗
February

Panagbenga Festival

📍 Baguio City, Luzon

The "Flower Festival" of Baguio fills the City of Pines with spectacular floats blanketed in fresh blooms, grand street dancing, and a month-long celebration of highland culture.

Flowers · Parade · Highland ↗
April (Holy Week)

Moriones Festival

📍 Marinduque, Luzon

During Holy Week, the island of Marinduque transforms as participants don elaborate Roman soldier masks and costumes to reenact the legend of Longinus — a uniquely Filipino passion play.

Religious · Theater · Masks ↗
January

Dinagyang Festival

📍 Iloilo City, Visayas

Iloilo's answer to Sinulog, Dinagyang is a UNESCO-recognized festival featuring warrior-themed dance troupes in elaborate body paint and costumes performing to thunderous drum beats.

UNESCO · Tribal Dance · Warriors ↗
October

Lanzones Festival

📍 Camiguin, Mindanao

Camiguin's beloved harvest festival where locals adorn themselves in lanzones fruit costumes and celebrate the island's famous sweet harvest with street dancing and trade fairs.

Harvest · Island · Tropical ↗
December

Giant Lantern Festival

📍 San Fernando, Pampanga, Luzon

The "Christmas Capital of the Philippines" hosts a dazzling competition of giant star-shaped lanterns — called parol — that spin and light up the night sky with kaleidoscopic brilliance.

Christmas · Lights · Parol ↗
